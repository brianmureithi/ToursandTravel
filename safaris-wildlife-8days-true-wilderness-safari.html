8 DAYS TRUE WILDERNESS SAFARI

Experience the best wilderness experience as you visit some of the best National parks and game reserves in Kenya. Track and experience the Big Five, spot the migration if you visit during the Migration period and get to embrace some of the true and explicit Kenyan Cultures.


 

Your Itinerary

Day 1: Masai Mara Game Reserve

You will be picked from your hotel and transferred to Wilson airport for your schedule flight to Masai Mara. The Masai Mara is about 45minutes flight. Upon arrival you will be met and transferred to Sand Rivers Mara Camp with game drive en-route. Maasai Mara is an extension of the Serengeti ecosystem to the south. Game abounds here, particularly when the migration is on, and millions of wildebeests and zebras make their way back and forth across the Mara River on their annual trek. The Mara is also noted for its abundance of lions - particularly the superb black maned males. Look out to see lots of elephants here often in large herds, as well as - if you're lucky - leopard, cheetah and rhino. All of the Big Five are residents. You're likely to see the African buffalo. Get the cameras ready and enjoy yourselves!


Meals: B, L, D | Accommodation: Sand Rivers Mara  

Day 2: Full Day Masai Mara Game Reserve

On your second day in the Mara, there's plenty of time to relax and fully appreciate the rich landscape. See the giant herds, but take time to admire the enchanting interplays that are eternally being played out. Boisterous young males fight for mating rights. Tiny zebra foals are adoringly nurtured by vigilant mothers. Bull elephants flap their ears in pride defiance, refusing to leave the waterhole before their extended family has had enough to drink. In some parks there is a preoccupation with spotting the Big Five. In the Maasai Mara there is so much going on that any tick-list goes out of the window


Meals: B, L, D | Accommodation: Sand Rivers Mara  

Day 3: Lake Nakuru National Park

After breakfast and your early morning game drive, you will be transferred to the airstrip for your flight out of Masai Mara to the Lovely Lake Nakuru National Park. Lake Nakuru is far more than a bird lover's paradise. The big cats will need no introduction after the Maasai Mara, nor will the large antelope herds. Flamingos provide a dramatic welcome to Lake Nakuru, over a million of them wading in the shallows and turning the lake shore a vivid pink. Over 400 species of bird offer a meandering soundtrack; kingfishers and hamerkops sing from the treetops, while cormorants and heron squawk from the shallows. Down besides the lakeshore the sheer numbers of flamingos defy your eyes, and their brilliant pink finds another contrast; this time the sharp black and white stripes of zebra herds.


Meals: B, L, D | Accommodation: Lake Nakuru Sopa Lodge  

Day 4: Amboseli National Park

After breakfast, you will be transferred to the Wilson airport for your maiden schedule flight to Amboseli National Park. Africa's highest mountain provides the backdrop to Amboseli, the snow-capped Uhuru Peak clearly visible above the flat savannah. Great elephant herds roam across this land, their distinct shapes providing a wonderful contrast on any photo of Mount Kilimanjaro. On one hand Amboseli offers a small and compact safari experience, the bountiful wildlife easily found congregating around swamps and dried out lake beds. Yet it's also a spacious place of serene beauty. Little compares to relaxing on a verandah and watching the elephants roam beneath Mount Kilimanjaro. On arrival you will be met and transferred to Tortillis camp for your lunch and later in the afternoon relaxation and an evening Game Drive before returning to camp for your dinner.


Meals: B, L, D | Accommodation: Elewana Tortilis Camp  

Day 5: Amboseli National Park

Today you spend the whole day exploring and experiencing the different landscapes, wildlife, birds and flora that Amboseli National Park has in store for you. On clear skies you are bound to see Mount Kilimanjaro on the horizon. Amboseli National Park is famous for hosting Africa's largest elephants and can be explored via day and night game drives, sundowner walks with Masai guides and a visit to the traditional local village.


Meals: B, L, D | Accommodation: Elewana Tortilis Camp  

Day 6: Tsavo West National Park

After breakfast you will be transferred to the local airstrip and fly out to Tsavo West National Park. Tsavo West has a variety of landscapes, from swamps and natural springs to rocky peaks, extinct volcanic cones, to rolling plains and sharp reddish outcrops. Wildlife can be difficult to spot because of the dense scrubs. In May 1948, a month after its conception Tsavo National Park was divided into East and West for administrative purposes. At 21 812 square km, Tsavo National Park is the largest park in Kenya. On arrival you will be met and transferred to Severin Safari Camp, you will freshen up, enjoy your barbequed lunch before heading out for your afternoon / evening game drive.


Meals: B, L, D | Accommodation: Finch Hattons Camp  

Day 7:Tsavo West National Park

The day starts with an early morning game drive along the springs of the Tsavo River – the lifeline for hippos and crocodiles. On your return to the camp breakfast waits. In the late afternoon, you will be taken to the highlight of the day. At Poacher’s Lookout, you will enjoy the beautiful sunset while having a glass of sparkling wine and canapés. From here, you have a beautiful view over the bush land of El Turesh. On the way back to the camp you experience the African savannah at night – a memorable adventure.


Meals: B, L, D | Accommodation: Finch Hattons Camp  

Day 8: Depart for Nairobi

A very early morning game drive, thereafter breakfast, today marks your last day on the confines of the Kenyan Wilderness. You will transferred to your local airstrip and fly out to Wilson Airport, marking the end of your short and memorable African safari. On landing at the Wilson Airport, you will be transferred to Ole Sereni Hotel for your day room and later on to the JKIA (Jomo Kenyatta International Airport) for your international flight. Lunch will be on your own account.


Meals: B,L
